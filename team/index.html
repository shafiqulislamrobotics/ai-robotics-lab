PRINCIPAL INVESTIGATOR

Dr. Shafiqul Islam

Xavier University of Louisiana

AI / Machine Learning Robotics Autonomous Systems Climate Computing Top 2% Scientist

Research Interests

Artificial Intelligence (Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Reinforcement Learning, Large Language Models, and Agentic AI), Robotics, Autonomous Systems, Computational Biology, and Climate Computing.

Biography

Dr. Islam's research focuses on AI-driven robotics, mechatronics, autonomous systems, adaptive and machine learning-based control for smart grid networks, and ML/DL applications in environmental monitoring and computational network systems.

With more than 28 years of experience in higher education, Dr. Islam has developed and taught a wide range of courses spanning Robotics, Mechatronics, Autonomous Systems, Electrical and Electronics Engineering, Computer Science, and Engineering disciplines.

His research has been supported by NASA, ONR/Navy, NSF, NSERC Canada, Harvard University, Carleton University, Khalifa University, MEXT Japan, and many other governmental, industrial, and academic organizations. He is the author and co-author of more than 110 peer-reviewed publications.
