About the Lab

AI for Robotics, Autonomous Systems, Computational Biology & Climate Computing Research Laboratory

Our Purpose

Mission Statement

Our laboratory develops intelligent systems that integrate theoretical models with artificial intelligence — Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Reinforcement Learning, Adaptive Learning, LLM, and Agentic AI — for robotics, autonomous systems, biological systems and climate change, to create technologies capable of solving complex scientific and real-world engineering problems.

Our Aim

Vision

Leading interdisciplinary research and advancing the future of AI-driven intelligent robotics, autonomous systems, and AI-driven healthcare and climate technologies.

Core Research Philosophy

  • Interdisciplinary AI research
  • Theory with real-world deployment
  • Scientific innovation and dissemination
  • Scalable robotics & autonomous intelligence
  • Ethical and trustworthy AI
Infrastructure

Laboratory Facilities

Our three state-of-the-art labs are equipped with advanced experimental testbeds containing the following setup for research and project-based teaching courses.

🚁

Autonomous Systems Lab

  • Autonomous drone fleet for swarming
  • Fleet of self-driving car modules
  • Fleet of mobile robotic systems
  • Helicopter VTOL platforms
🤖

Robotics & Mechatronics Lab

  • Advanced motion capture systems
  • Industrial robotic systems
  • Haptics & virtual reality systems
  • Quanser platforms (Servo, Pendulum, QUBE)
  • 2/3 DOF Helicopter, 2/4 DOF Planar manipulators
🛰

Climate Computing Lab

  • NASA Global Climate Computing Networks
  • GPM, AERONET & PANDORA systems
  • GPU servers for ML/Deep Learning
  • Embedded AI hardware & sensing
